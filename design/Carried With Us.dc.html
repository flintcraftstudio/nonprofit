Why we're here

We started Carried With Us because we have lived this. We are two mothers who know what it is to leave a hospital with empty arms — to plan a goodbye instead of a nursery, and to wonder how the world keeps turning.

For a long time, we each believed we were the only one. We weren't — and neither are you. This is the community we needed and couldn't find: a place where your child is remembered by name, where your grief is never too much, and where hope is allowed to sit quietly beside the sorrow. However long it has been, and however today is going, there is room for you here.

Come in, whenever you're ready

There's no right way and no right order. These are just gentle doors — open the one that feels possible today.

Our story

Before we were anything else, we were two mothers missing our children.

Carried With Us was not built in a boardroom. It was built on kitchen floors, in the small hours, out of the thing we each went looking for and couldn't find.

portrait — Sarah, golden hour

Sarah

Co-founder · mother to Grace

I lost my daughter, Grace, three days after she was born. For months, I couldn't say her name out loud without the whole room going quiet.

What carried me wasn't advice. It was the friend who sat with me on the kitchen floor and didn't try to fix anything — who just stayed. That is the feeling we've tried to build into everything here.

Carried With Us is the place I went looking for at 2am and couldn't find. So we made it.

"We couldn't find the place we needed. So we built it — for you, and for the children we carry with us."

Emily

Co-founder · mother to Theo

I'm Emily. My son, Theo, was stillborn at thirty-nine weeks. We had a name, a nursery, a whole life imagined. And then we came home to a silent house.

For a long time I thought grief was something you were supposed to get over. I've learned it's something you learn to carry — and that it's lighter when someone helps you carry it.

Sarah and I met in the worst year of our lives. This is what we built out of it, so no one else has to look as hard as we did.

Why we exist

The loss of a baby or child is one of the loneliest things a person can live through. It shouldn't also be one of the most invisible.

So we're building something steady and warm: a podcast, a community, resources, and gatherings — all held together by one belief. That your child mattered. That your grief makes sense. And that hope and heartbreak can share the same room, for as long as you need them to.

If tonight is heavy

Some of us arrive here in the rawest part of grief. If you are struggling to stay safe right now, please reach out — in the U.S. you can call or text 988 for the Suicide & Crisis Lifeline, any hour of any day. You do not have to carry tonight alone.
